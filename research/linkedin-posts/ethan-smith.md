Key Takeaways
* Mobile accounts for 75-86% of AI usage and is excluded from most 
  published data, making the real number 4-5x larger than what is 
  commonly cited.
* AI is already over half the size of search worldwide, up 300% 
  year over year. ChatGPT owns 86% of the AI market.
* The pie is getting bigger, not redistributing. Search has not 
  declined. The combined channel is up 16% in the US and 26% worldwide.

Long Tail Prompt Mismatch
* Average Google search is 3.5 words. Over 60% of ChatGPT prompts 
  are 10+ words. Marketers are still tracking ~7-word prompts in AEO 
  tools, measuring the head of a channel that is almost entirely tail.
* The long tail of AI prompts has almost no content competing for it.

Prompt Discovery Gap
* ChatGPT does not share prompt data. The only way to find out what 
  people are actually asking is to look where they already ask: sales 
  calls, support tickets, Reddit threads.
* Most marketing teams have no strategy built around this despite it 
  being the majority of AI activity.

Distributed Citations and Consensus Building
* 95% of cited domains fall outside the top ten. Reddit is number 
  one at 2.36% of all citations.
* AI synthesizes consensus. Being mentioned across many sources 
  matters more than ranking first in one. Volume and distribution 
  beat depth in a single channel.

Content Refresh Is the Most Ignored Growth Lever
* Stale content gets cited significantly less than fresh content. 
  For teams with existing content libraries, refreshing pages is 
  likely higher ROI than creating new ones.
* For technical content, refresh quality depends on accuracy: correct 
  model names, valid code samples, and current documentation 
  references. This can be built as a recurring automated workflow.

Sales Call Signals & Opportunities
* Customer questions in sales calls, support tickets, and call 
  recordings contain the long-tail prompts AI is being asked.
* Frontier questions, triggered by new launches or competitor moves, 
  often have zero content on the web. Being first to answer them is 
  a structural advantage that most teams are not pursuing.

ChatGPT Now Has 20% Share Of Search Traffic Worldwide

* For years, Google has controlled the search and discovery market. 
  For the first time in over a decade, Google's share of the search 
  and discovery market has shifted.
* Worldwide, Google's traffic share has decreased from 89% in 2023 
  to 71% in Q4 2025. ChatGPT now commands 19.5% of search worldwide, 
  considering web and app usage and adjusting for only asking prompts.
* In the US, Google's market share decreased from 88% in 2023 to 
  75%. ChatGPT has 12% traffic share.
* However, people are not using ChatGPT instead of Google or AI 
  instead of search. There is no decrease in visits to Google or 
  search. Instead, the pie is getting bigger.
* Worldwide search-related sessions have increased by 26% worldwide 
  and 16% in the US (comparing Q1 2023 vs. Q4 2025).
* These traffic numbers differ from other studies that estimate that 
  ChatGPT accounts for 3%-10% of search. This study includes mobile 
  app sessions, whereas other studies only include web visits. 
  However, 83% of AI sessions worldwide are on mobile apps, and 
  75% in the US.

Method
* Full Study: https://lnkd.in/dCVNv4nw
* Search & AI: To compare Google, search and other LLMs, we combine 
  Google Search with Gemini for asking prompts only. For ChatGPT, we 
  consider web and app visits adjusted for asking only prompts. For 
  other AI, we combine Perplexity, Grok, and Claude for asking 
  prompts only. For other search we combine Yahoo, Bing, DuckDuckGo, 
  and for worldwide we also include Baidu and Yandex.
* Prompts by User Intent: To adjust for Asking prompts, we use data 
  from the ChatGPT + Harvard study, "How People Use ChatGPT" between 
  July 2024 and June 2025. Not all Asking prompts are equivalent to 
  search, so this estimate is an upper bound.

AI Is Much Bigger Than You Think

I originally hypothesized that AI was over hyped and the size of 
its growth curve overstated. So, I sought to evaluate and likely 
disprove the prevailing narrative about the size of AI. However, 
what I found is that the size and trajectory of AI are *understated* 
and that AI is much bigger than I thought.

Study: https://lnkd.in/dCVNv4nw

Key Findings
1. Monthly sessions of AI are now 56% the size of search worldwide 
   and 34% in the US, 4x-5x larger than previous reports that only 
   include web data.
2. AI now receives 45B monthly sessions worldwide, and 5.4B monthly 
   sessions in the US.
3. Search-related sessions of AI (Asking prompts) are now 28% the 
   size of search worldwide and 17% in the US.
4. AI has grown even larger across the World than in the US, with 
   worldwide sessions 7x that of the US.
5. Search has not decreased, and neither has Google. Instead, the 
   pie has gotten bigger. Total search-related sessions, combining 
   search engines and search on LLMs, have increased by 26% worldwide 
   and by 16% in the US, comparing 2025 with 2024.
6. 83% of AI usage occurs in mobile apps worldwide, and 75% in the US.

Study & Method
* Metric: I compared web visits for the top 6 search engines 
  (Google, Bing, Yahoo, DuckDuckGo, Yandex, Baidu) with web visits 
  + mobile app sessions for the top 5 LLMs (ChatGPT, Gemini, 
  Perplexity, Grok, Claude).
* New Information: Most other public data showing the growth of AI 
  has only included web visits for LLMs. However, 75%-86% of usage 
  is on mobile apps rather than web. So, all other studies have 
  understated the actual size of AI.
* Source: Similarweb.
* Reproducibility: I include all the raw data, except for links to 
  Similarweb sources, so anyone can independently verify the accuracy 
  of the information (please do).
* Visits vs. Sessions: I did not use pageviews because I do not have 
  reliable pageviews for mobile apps. I did not use unique visitors 
  because you cannot deduplicate visitors across apps. So, I combined 
  visits and sessions. Note: these two metrics have slightly different 
  definitions.
