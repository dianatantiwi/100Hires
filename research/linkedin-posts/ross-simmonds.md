Most teams treat AI visibility as a new channel.

Wrong frame.

AI visibility is the compounded return on years of distribution 
decisions. SEO. Community participation. Review management. Guest 
contributions. Independent comparisons on sites you don't own.

Large language models pull from your entire digital footprint. Every 
review, community mention, guest post, and independent comparison 
feeds the answer they give about you. Your homepage is one input 
of hundreds.

That footprint is your real competitive moat.

G2's new research shows the buyer journey has split:
→ AI dominates early discovery
→ Review platforms gain influence as buyers evaluate and decide
→ Owned content matters most during retention and expansion

Your strategy can't stop at top-of-funnel awareness.

You need a full-stack approach:

Owned properties that clearly explain your positioning
Third-party mentions that validate your claims
Customer voice that reinforces credibility
Consistent messaging across every profile, directory, and community

Strategic work. Requires structure. The instruction underneath it 
is simple though: build trust signals where your buyers and the 
models advising them already look.

Five years from now, the most trusted brands will win. Full stop. 
Volume and noise stop mattering. Trust becomes the signal.

The most indispensable person on your marketing team probably 
doesn't have "engineer" in their title.

They're the one who built the automated content audit.
The one who connected your CRM to your reporting dashboard.
The one who figured out how to manage 500 product pages without 
hiring three more people.

Profound just gave that person a name: Marketing Engineer.

We've seen this pattern before with content engineering, treating 
content like infrastructure instead of a series of campaigns. When 
you systematize research, metadata, refresh cycles and distribution, 
output increases without chaos.

Now apply that mindset across the entire marketing org:

Competitive intelligence.
Sales enablement.
PR monitoring.
Review generation.

The opportunity for small and midsize teams is massive. Instead of 
hiring for every new channel, hire or develop one builder who creates 
leverage across all of them.

If you don't know where to start, begin with your content operations. 
Document the workflow. Circle the repetitive steps. Automate those first.

Strategy stays human. Systems scale the rest. That's the model.
